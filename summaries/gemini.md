This project proposal from Air University details the **Bionic Vision System**, an affordable, wearable assistive technology designed to enhance independent mobility for visually impaired individuals. The system consists of lightweight smart glasses equipped with an ESP32-S3 microcontroller and camera, wirelessly paired with an Android smartphone application that serves as the system's processing core.

Addressing the high costs and latency limitations of existing cloud-dependent or short-range mobility aids, this offline system processes visual data entirely on-device. It bridges the gap between macro-level GPS routing and micro-level navigation through a multi-stage AI pipeline. Key operational capabilities include ground and overhead hazard detection using an optimized YOLOv8 model, dynamic obstacle tracking via DeepSORT, monocular depth estimation for proximity zoning, and context-aware multilingual text recognition via EasyOCR.

A prioritized decision-making engine processes environmental data, delivering clear, step-by-step navigation cues through a low-latency Text-to-Speech interface to prevent user panic and information overload. To optimize performance, heterogeneous processing distributes workloads across the smartphone's CPU, GPU, and NPU. Documented limitations include high battery consumption, reliance on mid-to-high-end smartphones, and susceptibility to poor lighting or adverse weather.

This is a Final Year Project proposal from Air University, Islamabad for a "Bionic Vision System" — a wearable assistive technology for visually impaired individuals, developed by three BS Artificial Intelligence students under supervision of Sir Adil Sheraz and Mam Qurat-ul-ain.

The system addresses limitations of existing aids (white canes, Envision Glasses, OrCam, Seeing AI) which are costly, offer limited situational awareness, and fail to detect hanging objects, fast-moving hazards, or road signs. The proposed solution pairs ESP32-S3-based smart glasses (with OV2640 camera) with an Android app that processes video locally for low-latency, offline operation.

The system comprises eleven modules: data acquisition/preprocessing, YOLOv8-based hazard detection, dynamic obstacle tracking (using DeepSORT and rate-of-expansion calculations), proximity estimation via bounding-box area ratios, risk assessment scoring, decision/instruction generation with a decision-tree and cooldown logic, OCR-based warning sign understanding, voice interaction, manual button controls, macro-navigation via map APIs, and system integration using heterogeneous mobile processing (NPU/GPU/CPU).

Limitations include lighting dependency, high battery usage, need for mid-to-high-end smartphones, weather sensitivity, and limited wireless range between glasses and phone. The document also includes a literature review, tools/technologies list, stakeholder roles, module-based work division among the three students, a WBS/Gantt chart spanning roughly a year, UI mockups, and references.

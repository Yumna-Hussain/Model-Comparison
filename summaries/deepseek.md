The Bionic Vision System is a proposed wearable assistive technology designed to aid visually impaired individuals with independent navigation. It consists of smart glasses with a camera connected to a mobile application that processes visual data in real-time. The system aims to bridge macro-navigation (GPS routing) and micro-navigation (hazard detection) by identifying static and dynamic obstacles, estimating their distance and direction, and providing clear audio guidance. Key features include deep learning-based hazard detection, depth estimation, and context-aware OCR for reading warning signs.

The system is comprised of eleven functional modules, covering data acquisition, hazard detection, obstacle tracking, proximity estimation, risk assessment, decision making, OCR, user interaction, macro navigation, and system integration. It prioritizes low-latency, on-device AI processing using TensorFlow Lite to avoid cloud dependency. While the system promises to be a cost-effective and comprehensive solution, it has limitations, including dependency on good lighting, high smartphone battery consumption, and the need for a modern mobile device. The project's development is divided among three team members, with a structured timeline for analysis, design, development, testing, and final delivery.
